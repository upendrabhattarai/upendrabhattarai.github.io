About me :

I’m a PhD student with Prof. Neil Gemmell, Prof. Robert Poulin, and Dr Eddy Dowle at the University of Otago, Dunedin. I am studying the mechanisms of mind-altering parasites with a particular focus on water-seeking behaviour induced by mermithid nematodes on their arthropod hosts. Among other things, my research focuses on genome assembly and annotation, comparative RNA-seq and bisulphite-seq analysis and comparative genomic analysis of both the host and the parasite.
Before starting at the University of Otago, I completed my first PhD at the Northwest A&F University, China under the supervision of Prof. David Wang. My research focused on examining the mechanisms of tree-top and hyperactivity behaviour in lepidopteran larvae induced by baculoviruses through RNA-seq analysis. We found that the phototransduction pathway and circadian cycles play key roles in such behaviour changes. I completed my Master degree from the Norwegian University of Life Sciences, Norway, studying the effect of acaropathogenic fungi, Neozygites floridana on the sexual behaviour of two-spotted spider mites under the supervision of Dr Ingeborg Klingen and Dr Nina Trandem. During and after my Masters I worked as a Research Assistant at the Norwegian Institute of Bioeconomy Research (NIBIO) for a year working on a variety of projects involving experiments with fungi, mites, and flies. I got my Bachelor degree in Agriculture from Tribhuvan University, Nepal.
